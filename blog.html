The Art of Finishing: How Dhoni Rewrote the ODI Playbook

By Ravi Shastri | Special Cricket Correspondent

RANCHI — In the history of limited-overs cricket, few figures have commanded the game quite like Mahendra Singh Dhoni. Emerging from the small town of Ranchi with a fearless batting style and long hair, he evolved into the ultimate mastermind of modern cricket, redefined the role of a wicketkeeper-batsman, and became the world's most trusted finisher.

Dhoni's ability to take matches down to the very final over, calculated to the last run, became his trademark signature. "It's about who blinks first," Dhoni famously noted regarding his high-stakes ice-cool composure during tense run-chases, an approach that consistently left opposing bowlers second-guessing themselves.

The Historic 2011 World Cup Triumph

It remains the defining image of Indian cricket in the 21st century: Dhoni stepping down the track and hitting a massive six into the Mumbai night sky. Promoted up the batting order in the final against Sri Lanka, his unbeaten 91* ended a 28-year World Cup drought for India, cementing his legacy as a legendary big-match player.
